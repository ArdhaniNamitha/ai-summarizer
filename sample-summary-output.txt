## Formula One


### Formula Racing Pinnacle

- Formula One is the highest class of worldwide racing for open-wheel single-seater formula racing cars sanctioned by the Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile (FIA) Formula One cars are the world's fastest regulated road-course racing cars, owing to high cornering speeds achieved by generating large amounts of aerodynamic downforce 
- A Formula One season consists of a series of races, known as Grands Prix 
- A points scoring system is used to determine two annual World Championships: one for drivers, and one for the constructors 
- The Formula One World Championship is considered the pinnacle of motorsport and is considered to be the pinnacle 


### Formula Group Hub

- The word formula in the name refers to the set of rules all participant cars must follow 
- The Formula One Group is owned by Liberty Media, which acquired it in 2017 from private-equity firm CVC Capital Partners for US$8 billion 
- The United Kingdom is the hub of Formula One racing with six out of ten teams based there 
- The average annual cost of running a Formula One team is approximately £220 million 


### 1950 Romeo Won

- The first Formula One race took place at the 1950 British Grand Prix at Silverstone Circuit in the United Kingdom on 13 May 1950 
- Giuseppe Farina, competing for Alfa Romeo, won the first Drivers' World Championship, narrowly defeating his teammate Juan Manuel Fangio 
- Stirling Moss, despite being regarded as one of the greatest drivers in the 1950s and 1960s, never won the Formula One championship 
- This era featured teams managed by road-car manufacturers, such as Alfa Romeo, Ferrari, Mercedes-Benz and Maserati 


### Formula Seasons Held

- The 1952 and 1953 seasons were run to Formula Two regulations, for smaller, less powerful cars, due to concerns over the dearth of Formula One cars 
- The 1954 Formula One world championship was won by Mercedes Benz, before the team withdrew from all motorsport competitions due to the 1955 Le Mans disaster 
- Promoters held non-championship Formula One events for many years, with the last of these being held in 1983
